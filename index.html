Virus spread progress chart

The below scene provides a high level view on the spread progress of the COVID-19 virus; The chart displays the top 10 countries based on the number of the recorded cases.

The data is displayed in the form of a chart that runs over time & keeps updating the total number of cases per country over time; It provides a simple visual aid for a quick glance on where the virus started spreading & how it progressed over time till the current time where we can find out easily what are the current top 10 countries in terms of number of recorded cases.

Fatality ratio progress

In the below scene we display the fatality ratio per country versus number of confirmed cases since the beginning of the spread of the COVID 19 virus.

The scatter plot represents each country in the form of a circle & circles are color coded with different colors that represent the continent where a country belongs to.

Hitting play will go back to the beginning of recording data of the panademic & start showing the progress till our current date; you could pause at anytime by clicking pause & you could reset the animation by clicking reset at anytime. More details could be provided if you hover over a specific circle of interest to get stats like fatality ratio, confirmed cases, total deaths & total recovery.

Some insights could be dedcuted from the below scatter plot; for example It could be seen that currently Yemen holds the highest fatality ratio; followed by some Western European countries as Belgium, Italy, UK & out of North America Mexico is leading in fatality ratio at the current time.

Per country progress charts

This scene provides more insights per country with an optional parameter to select a country so you can drill down into more details.

On selection of one country from the drop down menu, that would display the curves for the selected country over time starting from the date when the panademic has started till our current date. Charts displayed are for the confirmed cases, death cases & recovery cases, each displayed as total recorded so far till specific date. These charts could help getting an idea of the progress of the spread.
